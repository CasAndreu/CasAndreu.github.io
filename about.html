Andreu Casas

Political Science Graduate Student

I am a graduate student in the Department of Political Science at the University of Washington. I am also a La Caixa Fellow and a research fellow at the Center for American Politics and Public Policy. My research interests encompass the areas of political representation, legislative politics, political communication, social movements, interest groups, text analysis and computational social science. I am especially interested in how social movements, interest groups, and expert bureaucrats influence the political agenda and the decision making process. Prior to starting graduate school, I received a B.A. in political science from the University of Barcelona and I worked as a research assistant of the Spanish Policy Agendas Project.

Outside the academic sphere, I love all kinds of sports and music. I played cello for many years and I like listening to Schoenberg and Kodaly. I specially love Starker's recording of Kodaly's cello solo sonata.
